Writing Portfolio

The Michigan Daily: Daily Arts Writer

Published works written for The Michigan Daily, which has a circulation of 16,000 and is the largest circulating paper in Washtenaw County. Click on the individual titles for article links.

THE INVISIBLE MUSICIAN: CARILLONISTS AT MICHIGAN

A self-pitched longform piece on what it's like to be a carillonist. The story started out because I wanted to write a piece on women of color in higher education, and then evolved to have a more specific focus after I talked with Professor Ng about her experiences.

CREATIVE HEALING: THE RESTORATIVE POWER OF ART, MUSIC AND DRAMA

The lead article for the Daily Arts March 2017 B-side on Mental Heath. I interviewed two local art therapists and one drama therapist on what art therapy involves and its untapped benefits.

MODERN PAGANISM: DEFINING A NEW NORMAL

The lead for Daily Art's October 2017 "Spooky" B-side. I interviewed a witch and a druid about their beliefs, practices, and how Paganism has developed in the 21st century.

ARTIST PROFILE: MAIZE MIRCHI

This artist profile on the University's own Maize Mirchi, a South Asian themed competitive acapella group, is part of Daily Arts's January 2018 International themed B-Side.

FESTIVAL REPORT: LOLLAPALOOZA 2017

A runthrough and review of my experience at Lollapalooza Chicago 2017. I was the first person in the history of the paper approved for a Lollapalooza press credential.

VIET THANH NGUYEN HUMANIZES IN 'THE REFUGEES'

A book review of Viet Thanh Nguyen's collection of short stories, "The Refugees."
